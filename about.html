About Diane

A Technical Foundation with a Community Heart

I'm Diane Peterson and I believe that the organizations making the most difference in our community deserve the highest quality support. My journey to founding Anacortes Nonprofit Navigator has been a 25-year path of combining technical expertise with a passion for local service.

Diane Peterson, Founder

Background & Experience

With a B.S. in Computer Science and an MBA, I spent the early part of my career as a consultant building complex systems for corporations. This phase of my life taught me the power of robust infrastructure and the efficiency that comes from well-designed technology. It also taught me every client is unique, and the best solution is the one that fits their specific needs and constraints.

After shifting my focus to raising my family and volunteering locally, I returned to the professional world with a new mission: serving the organizations that hold our community together.

Community Involvement

Aside from volunteering, my recent employment in the nonprofit sector includes:

Fidalgo DanceWorks: For six years, I managed the administrative and operational side of FDW. Working alongside a talented team of dance teachers and directors, I managed the ins and outs of the business: running everything from IT and CRM management to bookkeeping and events.

Skagit Community Foundation: My time at SCF gave me a broader perspective on our local nonprofit landscape. Many dedicated leaders stuggle with operations not because they lack vision, but because they lack time, budget, or specialized expertise.

Approach & Philosophy

I'm not just a consultant—I'm your partner. There's no one-size-fits-all solution. Every organization has unique constraints in budget, staff capacity, and existing infrastucture. I thrive on the challenge of finding practical solutions that work within each organization's reality. We'll work together to find what fits your needs now and into the future.

My greatest satisfaction comes from when the systems I build take the "tech stress" off your plate, so you can focus on serving our community.

Core Values

  • Service: Community needs first
  • Clarity: Practical systems
  • Sustainability: Solutions that last
