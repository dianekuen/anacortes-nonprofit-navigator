About Me

A Technical Foundation with a Community Heart

I believe that the organizations making the most difference in our community deserve the highest quality support. My journey to founding Anacortes Nonprofit Navigator has been a 20-year path of combining technical expertise with a passion for local service.

Background & Experience

With a Computer Science degree and an MBA, I spent the early part of my career as a consultant building complex systems for corporations. This phase of my life taught me the power of robust infrastructure and the efficiency that comes from well-designed technology.

After shifting my focus to raising my family and volunteering locally, I returned to the professional world with a new mission: serving the organizations that hold our community together.

Community Involvement

Fidalgo DanceWorks: I managed all administrative and operational systems for this local arts organization, building their infrastructure from the ground up—from IT and CRM to bookkeeping and events.

Skagit Community Foundation: My time here gave me insight into our nonprofit landscape. I saw firsthand that many dedicated leaders struggle with operations not because they lack vision, but because they lack time, budget, or specialized expertise.

Approach & Philosophy

I'm not just a consultant—I'm your partner. There's no one-size-fits-all solution. Every organization works within constraints: budget, staff, existing systems. We'll work together to find what fits your needs now and into the future.

I understand that in small nonprofits, you're wearing many hats at once. My goal is to take the "tech stress" off your plate, creating sustainable systems that let you focus on your mission.

Core Values

  • Service: Community needs first
  • Clarity: Practical systems
  • Sustainability: Solutions that last
